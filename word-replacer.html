How to Use the Word Replacer

1

Paste Your Text

Paste the text you want to edit into the Input Text box. This could be a blog article, a code file, an email template, a legal document—anything that needs word or phrase substitutions. Use the Sample button to see a demonstration with example text.

2

Enter Find & Replace Terms

Type the word or phrase you want to find in the Find field. Enter the replacement text in the Replace with field. Leave Replace with blank to delete all occurrences of the search term without substituting anything.

3

Configure Matching Options

Toggle Case sensitive to match exact capitalisation. Enable Whole word only to avoid partial matches (e.g. "cat" won't match "concatenate"). Switch on Regex mode for advanced pattern matching using standard JavaScript regular expression syntax.

4

Click Replace All & Copy

Hit Replace All to substitute every match in one go. The output panel shows the edited text and a count of how many replacements were made. Use ↩ Undo to revert to the previous state, then Copy or Download your result.

↓ About This Tool

Why Use a Word Replacer Tool?

Manual find-and-replace in a word processor only scratches the surface. This tool gives you precise control over how and where substitutions happen.

⚡

Bulk Replacement in Seconds

Replace hundreds of occurrences of a word across a long document in one click. No scrolling, no missing instances, no risk of accidentally editing the wrong word. Every match is found and replaced consistently and instantly.

🔍

Whole-Word Matching

Prevent unintended partial matches. When whole-word mode is on, searching for "log" will not alter "login", "catalogue", or "dialog"—only the standalone word "log". Essential when working with code, technical writing, or any text with compound words.

🧩

Regex Pattern Support

Power users can write regular expressions to match complex patterns. Replace all email addresses, strip HTML tags, normalise phone number formats, or substitute entire sentence structures using capture groups. Standard JavaScript regex syntax is fully supported.

↩️

Instant Undo

Made a mistake? The Undo button reverts your last replacement instantly, restoring the previous output state. You can experiment freely with different search terms and replacement strings without worrying about losing your work.

📋

Replacement History

Every replacement you make is logged in the history panel showing what was found and what it was replaced with. This is useful when making sequential changes to a document and you need to track or audit what substitutions were applied.

🛡️

100% Private

All processing runs locally in your browser. Your text, search terms, and replacements are never transmitted to any server. You can safely use this tool with confidential documents, proprietary code, and sensitive business content.
